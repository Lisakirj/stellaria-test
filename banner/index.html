Important Safety Information Scroll ▼
◆ BOXED WARNING

Ion drive co-exposure within 6 hours of Stellaria administration has been associated with prolonged QT interval and, in rare cases, Torsades-class arrhythmia during cryo-induction.

Indication

Stellaria is indicated as hibernation-assist therapy in licensed deep-space personnel ≥ 21 years undergoing planned cryo-induction on missions of 90 days or longer.

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to morphanidine-C; active cryo-sensitization syndrome (CSS); uncorrected Grade II telomeric instability; Class-III temporal stabilizers; pregnancy confirmed or suspected at time of dosing.

Warnings and Precautions

Cardiac effects: see Boxed Warning. Hypothalamic set-point drift has been reported during first-exposure inductions; monitor core temperature for 90 minutes post-thaw. Neuro-vestibular effects, including transient orientation loss, may occur on re-animation.

Adverse Reactions

Most common adverse reactions (≥ 5%, vs. placebo) were dry mouth, paresthesia during re-animation, transient temporal displacement, metallic taste, and mild photosensitivity.

Drug Interactions

Avoid concurrent use with Class-III temporal stabilizers and monoamine oxidase inhibitors within 14 days. Ion drive co-exposure: see Boxed Warning.

Use in Specific Populations

Pregnancy: contraindicated. Pediatric use: safety and effectiveness not established in patients under 21 years. Reduce dose in moderate hepatic or renal impairment.
